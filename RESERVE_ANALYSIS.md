# PNG Reserve Status Analysis - India

## Strategic Importance of Reserves

Just like crude oil reserves, **LNG reserves serve as a critical buffer** against supply disruptions. India maintains LNG reserves at:

1. **LNG Import Terminals** - Storage tanks at regasification facilities
2. **Underground Storage** - Depleted gas fields used for strategic storage
3. **Pipeline Network** - Gas in transit through the distribution network
4. **Company Inventories** - PNG operators' working inventory

## Reserve Calculation & Metrics

### Current Reserve Status (March 2026)

**Reserve Days Formula:**
```
Reserve Days = (Total LNG in Storage / Daily Consumption) 
Reserve Days = (Total LNG in Storage / 45 MMTPA ÷ 365 days)
```

**Baseline Scenario:**
- Total Terminal Storage: ~3-4 days of supply
- Daily Consumption: ~123 MMSCMD (45 MMTPA ÷ 365)
- Reserve Capacity: ~369-492 MMSCM (3-4 days)

**Current Crisis Scenario (March 2026):**
- Import Decline: 44% (from 45 to 25 MMTPA)
- Daily Consumption: ~68 MMSCMD (25 MMTPA ÷ 365)
- Reserve Depletion Rate: Accelerated due to supply shortage
- **Reserve Days Remaining: 2-3 days** (CRITICAL)

### Strategic Reserve Policy (Proposed - Oct 2025)

India is mandating **10% additional storage capacity** at all LNG terminals:
- Current Total Capacity: 42.5 MMTPA
- 10% Buffer: 4.25 MMTPA
- **New Requirement: 46.75 MMTPA** (including buffer)

This 10% buffer would provide:
- **Additional 1-2 days of supply buffer**
- Protection against short-term disruptions
- Compliance with energy security standards

## Underground Storage Reserves

### Depleted Gas Fields for Storage

India has identified several depleted gas fields for strategic LNG storage:

1. **Bassein Field** (ONGC) - Western Offshore
   - Potential Storage: ~50-100 MMSCM
   - Status: Under evaluation
   - Timeline: 2-3 years for development

2. **Tapti Field** (ONGC) - Western Offshore
   - Potential Storage: ~30-50 MMSCM
   - Status: Feasibility study ongoing
   - Timeline: 3-4 years

3. **Rajahmundry Field** (ONGC) - Eastern Onshore
   - Potential Storage: ~20-30 MMSCM
   - Status: Planning phase
   - Timeline: 2-3 years

**Total Potential Underground Storage: ~100-180 MMSCM**

## Recommendations for Reserve Enhancement

### Short-term (0-6 months)
1. **Maximize Terminal Utilization** - Optimize storage at existing terminals
2. **Increase Spot Purchases** - Buy LNG on spot market despite high prices
3. **Activate Emergency Reserves** - Use all available storage capacity
4. **Demand Management** - Implement rationing for non-essential users

### Medium-term (6-18 months)
1. **Accelerate Underground Storage Development** - Fast-track depleted field projects
2. **Expand Terminal Capacity** - Complete Dhamra, Chhara, Jaigarh terminals
3. **Implement 10% Buffer Policy** - Mandate additional storage at all terminals
4. **Diversify Supply Sources** - Reduce Middle East dependency

### Long-term (18+ months)
1. **Develop Strategic Reserve Facilities** - Dedicated LNG storage like SPR for oil
2. **Build FSRU Capacity** - Flexible floating terminals for rapid deployment
3. **Interconnect Terminals** - Pipeline network for reserve pooling
4. **Regional Storage Hubs** - Distributed storage across India
